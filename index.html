What I actually learned

Not "leveraged AI to enhance UX." The real, recurring stuff across all four builds:

01

AI features fail differently than normal API calls — rate limits, token exhaustion, and slow or malformed responses all need their own handling, not a generic try/catch.

02

What you show the user when the AI call fails matters as much as what you show when it works. A silent failure or a raw error message is worse than the feature not existing.

03

Testing AI features costs real money and real time — you can't just re-run a suite hundreds of times the way you would with normal unit tests, so you learn to test smarter and mock more.

04

The AI feature is maybe 20% of the actual work. The other 80% is the same fundamentals I already knew — auth, schema design, state management — which is why 7 years of that background made these builds faster, not irrelevant.
